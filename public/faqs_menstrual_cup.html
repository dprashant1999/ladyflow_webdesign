Menstrual Cup

What is a menstrual cup and how does it work?

A menstrual cup is a reusable, bell-shaped silicone, rubber, or plastic device inserted into the vagina to collect menstrual blood. Unlike pads and tampons, which absorb the flow, the cup collects it, and it can be emptied, cleaned, and reused for up to 10 years.

How do I insert and remove a menstrual cup?

To insert, fold the cup and gently insert it into your vagina, where it will unfold and create a seal to collect menstrual fluid. To remove, pinch the base to break the seal and gently pull it out. Always wash your hands before inserting or removing.

How often should I empty my menstrual cup?

You should empty your menstrual cup every 8-12 hours, depending on your flow. On heavier days, you might need to empty it more frequently. Cups can hold more fluid than tampons, so many people find them more convenient.

Can I use a menstrual cup if I have a heavy flow?

Yes! Menstrual cups are suitable for all flow levels. Most cups can hold between 20-30ml of fluid, which is about twice as much as a regular tampon, making them a great option for heavy flow days.

Is it safe to wear a menstrual cup overnight?

Yes, it is safe to wear a menstrual cup overnight. Since it can be worn for up to 12 hours, you don't need to worry about overnight leakage or changing it while you sleep, as long as it's emptied before bedtime.

How do I clean and care for my menstrual cup?

Before and after each cycle, sterilize your menstrual cup by boiling it in water for 3-5 minutes. During your period, wash the cup with mild soap and water every time you empty it. Avoid using harsh chemicals or scented soaps.

Can I use a menstrual cup if I have an IUD?

Yes, but you need to be cautious. Consult your healthcare provider before using a menstrual cup with an IUD to ensure correct positioning and to avoid dislodging the IUD when removing the cup. Always break the suction seal before removing the cup.

Are menstrual cups safe and eco-friendly?

Yes, menstrual cups are made of medical-grade silicone or rubber, making them safe for your body. They are also eco-friendly because they are reusable and can last up to 10 years, significantly reducing menstrual product waste.

Can teens or first-time users use a menstrual cup?

Yes, teens and first-time users can use a menstrual cup. Some companies offer smaller sizes specifically designed for younger people or those with lighter flows. It may take some practice to get used to inserting and removing the cup.

What should I do if I experience discomfort while using a menstrual cup?

If you experience discomfort, try adjusting the position or checking the size of the cup. It might be too large or not positioned correctly. Ensure the stem is trimmed if it's too long. If discomfort persists, consult a healthcare professional.
